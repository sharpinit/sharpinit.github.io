FAQ

Your Questions.
Answered.

Still deciding? These are the questions we hear most before a first conversation.

Reach Out to Dr. Sharp
Do I need a gym membership to see you?
No. Your session fee covers access to the facility during our appointment. No existing membership required at either location.
Do I need a doctor's referral?
No referral needed. Idaho allows direct access to physical therapists, so you can reach out immediately. If imaging or a physician evaluation is warranted, I'll help guide you through that process.
Why don't you take insurance?
Insurance-based PT means shorter sessions, aides doing the work, and care restricted by a third party's rules. Operating out-of-network means I give you 60–90 minutes of fully individualized care with me — every time, without limitations.
Can I use my HSA or FSA?
Yes. Physical therapy is a qualified medical expense. HSA and FSA cards are accepted at time of service. Out-of-network superbills are available upon request for potential reimbursement through your insurance plan.
How many sessions will I need?
Because sessions are longer and entirely one-on-one, most clients reach their goals in fewer visits than a traditional clinic. We'll outline a realistic timeline during your initial evaluation so you always know what to expect.
I'm not a competitive athlete. Is this for me?
Absolutely. If you want to move without pain and feel confident in your body, you're an athlete in my eyes. Whether you're chasing a PR or just want to carry groceries without wincing, the fundamentals of great movement apply.
What if I'm in a lot of pain right now?
Acute pain is often the best time to come in. Hands-on care and guided gentle movement can calm the area down and accelerate recovery — far faster than waiting it out at home.
What's your cancellation policy?
Please provide at least 24 hours' notice to cancel or reschedule. I respect your time and ask the same in return.
